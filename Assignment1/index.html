Initial impression

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I paid attention to when interacting with broomstick.com was the colour palette used. The shades of blue, green, and grey make it easy to distinguish one element from another while creating cohesiveness through the page. This is supported by different textures and shapes within each colour which brings the page to life.

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • The first “slide” of the page contains a header with four clicking options that leads to different parts of the page.
  • Reading the small portions of text throughout the page.
  • Scrolling down the page and noticing animated elements.
  • The part of the page regarding “Paintings” slides sideways when I scroll downwards.
  • Only the last to “slides” of the page contains clicking options besides the header in the first slide. The heart to “send rays of kindness”, the dumpster with the sign “Clean where there is no littering!” and the option to share the page elsewhere.
  • It is possible to drag the trash on the last slide around, but they always fall back into their initial spot.
  • Scrolling sideways is not possible. The page moves a little to make it clear that it cannot move any further. Scrolling downwards is the only option.

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spend most time reading the story that the page presents while appreciating elements moving around to support it. I especially focused on the part of the page regarding what would happen to the planet without the wiper. I also spend time on trying to figure out why I could drag the trash around on the last slide and not put it in the dumpster to “help” the wiper.

What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action in my two minute interaction with Broomstick.com was scrolling down the page as this is the only way to access each slide and enable the moving elements of the page. If reading the story of the page counts as an action, then this would also be one of the most common.

Intended goals

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

My impression of the intended primary goal of Broomstick.com was to express the value of janitors and wipers and how most people take their work for granted. The last slide also explicitly states the “Purpose of the site” to be: “To show the importance and value of professions, so that “working” professions are not treated with prejudice, but on the contrary appreciate the labor of others”. So my impression of the primary goals aligns with the project authors intention.

How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

Broomstick.com communicates the primary goal by telling a story through the page. The story contains of historical facts, empathy appealing messages and conjectures about a world without the work of janitors and wipers. One of the visual effects I noticed is how the janitor on the first slide is completely unknown as his figure consists of grey checkers, which in the digital world can mean that this part is transparent. When the janitor is showed for the last time further down the page, he suddenly has the same texture as everyone else where his face, clothes, etc. are visible. This aligns with that specific part of the story, where the focus is on remembering to show gratitude for the janitorial profession.

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

My impression of how Broomstick.com should be interacted with over time is reflected in the statement on the last slide of the page saying: “The project was realized as part of a course on layout and animation”. Broomstick.com will probably not become a frequently used website, as the future use of the page is to read the same story again. It therefore makes sense that the website was a project in a course on layout and animation and that visual elements were the first I noticed about the page.

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

As mentioned in the above citation, Broomstick.com was created as a part of a course on layout and animation. This emphasizes my impression that the page does not invite multiple visits. However, it can be argued that certain elements of the page can make people revisit the website. One of these elements could be the historical facts regarding the janitorial profession. These might be useful later for the right person. Another element could be the combination of aesthetically pleasing visuals and the message of the page that makes it memorable and a place to revisit and show to others.

Media forms referenced

What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

Paper can be seen as a medium for communication, and multiple elements on Broomstick.com resembles paper. Most of the background is crumpled in the way paper crumples and some of the slides are created with a “scrapbook effect”, where it looks like it is a physical page ripped from somewhere else. Furthermore, a part of the website more explicitly resembles a newspaper with a headline and multiple news articles underneath. There are also digital media referenced on the website with possibility to send the project author a telegram or visit her profile on Instagram.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

The reference of the paper medium can be seen as supporting the main action of reading through the page - like reading a paper or a book.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The reference of reading a paper or a book communicates to me that I should be focused on taking the story in. I am reading this to understand it and remember it. The previous mentioned visual effect of the janitor being transparent at first and visible at the end also connects with the paper reference by enhancing the “storyline”.

Frustrating vs. Satisfying

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part of the interaction was on the last slide where there is a dumpster and a lot of trash. For the first time on the website, it is possible to drag the elements around which to me felt like I should be able to do something with them. It was also possible to open the dumpster and it seemed obvious that I was meant to drag the trash into the dumpster. This action was not possible – whatever I did, the elements kept reversing to their original spot. This was a little frustrating and the possibility to drag the items around seemed useless.

What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

To me the whole page is aesthetically pleasing which is satisfying by itself. A part of the page that I especially found satisfying was when trash was used at the bottom of one slide for the janitor to clean, and then “rains” from the top of the following slide over the Taj Mahal. That was a satisfying bridge between the two slides.
